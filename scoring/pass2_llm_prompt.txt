You are scoring a guided meditation transcript using the Contemplative Activity Taxonomy (CAT), a 10-dimensional framework for characterizing meditation practices based on their instruction text.

You will receive:
1. The full transcript of a guided meditation
2. Pass 1 keyword detection results (indicator counts and detected phrases)
3. Metadata (title, channel, duration)

Your task: score each of the 10 dimensions based on the TRANSCRIPT CONTENT (not the title or metadata). Use the Pass 1 indicators as supporting evidence but override them when the holistic reading of the transcript contradicts the keyword counts.

---

## Dimension Definitions and Anchors

### D1. Attentional Constraint (AC) [0-100]
How much does the instruction narrow the attentional aperture?
- 0: Total freedom. "Let the mind rest wherever it goes."
- 25: Soft field. "Notice whatever arises."
- 50: Guided field. "Move your attention through the body."
- 75: Firm anchor. "Return to the breath whenever the mind wanders."
- 100: Locked focus. "Hold attention on the flame without wavering."

### D2. Somatic Engagement (SE) [0-100]
How much does the practice recruit bodily awareness?
- 0: Purely cognitive. "Contemplate the meaning of impermanence."
- 50: Body as co-equal. "Observe breath and thoughts accompanying it."
- 100: Fully somatic. "Feel the weight of your body. Nothing else."

### D3. Startup Modality (categorical)
What initiates the meditative state in the first minutes?
- none: begins directly with main practice
- breath_regulation: pranayama, counted breaths
- body_scan: systematic attention to body regions
- physical_movement: walking, stretching, mudra
- relaxation_induction: progressive muscle relaxation, autogenic phrases

### D4. Object Nature (categorical, list)
Primary object(s) of attention as specified by the instruction.
Choose all that apply: somatic, verbal, visual, auditory, cognitive, affective, none_open

### D5. Object Density (OD) [0-100]
How many distinct objects of attention does the practice employ?
- 0: No object (open awareness)
- 20: Single object held throughout
- 50: Small set cycled through (e.g., metta: self, friend, neutral, difficult, all)
- 75: Many objects in sequence (body scan through 30+ regions)
- 100: Everything simultaneously ("notice everything")

### D6. Temporal Dynamics (TD) [0-100]
How much does the attentional task change over the course of the practice?
- 0: Completely static. Same instruction throughout.
- 50: Phased. Two or three distinct stages.
- 100: Rapid, unpredictable shifts.

### D7. Affective Cultivation (AffC) [0-100]
How much does the practice actively generate or cultivate emotional states?
- 0: Affect-neutral. "Simply observe."
- 50: Active cultivation alongside observation.
- 100: Intense affective generation. "Let devotion fill every cell."

### D8. Interoceptive Demand (ID) [0-100]
How much does the practice require sensitivity to internal bodily signals?
- 0: No interoceptive demand (mantra without body reference).
- 50: Moderate. "Notice rise and fall of the abdomen."
- 100: Maximal precision. "Detect the boundary between sensation and no-sensation."

### D9. Metacognitive Load (ML) [0-100]
How much does the practice ask the meditator to monitor their own mental process?
- 0: No self-monitoring. "Just repeat the mantra."
- 25: Light. "When you notice wandering, return."
- 50: Active. "Notice whether attention is stable or unstable."
- 75: Monitoring of monitoring. "Be aware of the quality of awareness itself."
- 100: Full recursive self-observation. "Watch the watcher."

### D10. Relational Orientation (RO) [0-100]
How much does the practice involve others, real or imagined?
- 0: Entirely solitary.
- 50: Specific others. "Bring to mind a loved one, a neutral person, a difficult person."
- 100: Fundamentally interpersonal.
